## Design Context

### Users
Mixed B2B audience — independent boutique hotel owners (cautious, personally invested), mid-size property managers (operationally minded, want efficiency), and enterprise/chain hotel staff (expect professional polish, compliance-aware). The form captures legal documents and Stripe bank details, so anxiety is a real factor at the high-stakes steps. The interface needs to work for a nervous first-timer and a seasoned ops manager equally well.

### Brand Personality
Trustworthy and reassuring above all. Users are handing over legal agreements, personal names, and bank credentials — they need to feel the platform is serious, secure, and stable. Secondary: efficient and precise. Don't waste their time with decorative noise. Three-word personality: **reliable, polished, calm**.

### Aesthetic Direction
A considered blend of Linear/Vercel (dark, sharp, trend-forward, precisely minimal) and Stripe (business-focused, professional B2B, strong typographic hierarchy). Dark mode as default — this is a business tool, operators use it in hotel office environments. Accent color is blue (already established). No warm hospitality softness (not Airbnb), no enterprise sterility (not Salesforce). The reference is "a design-forward SaaS that a hospitality business would trust with their money."

Anti-references: generic admin UIs, glassmorphism, cyan-on-dark AI aesthetics, over-decorated dashboards.

### Design Principles
1. **Anxiety reduction at high-stakes moments** — legal signing and Stripe steps need extra reassurance: clear progress, visible save states, undo paths, no ambiguity about what was committed.
2. **Precision over decoration** — every element earns its place. Whitespace as a feature, not filler.
3. **Hierarchy over uniformity** — typography and spacing should create clear visual flow, not uniform padding everywhere.
4. **Trust through density control** — forms should never feel overwhelming. One clear task per section, progressive disclosure for complexity.
5. **Business-grade polish** — the interface should signal that OpenBookings is a premium platform worth the partnership.
